Clive Staples Lewis

From atheism to apologist of the Christian faith

C. S. Lewis | photo: National Portrait Gallery, London

Here's a time line of Lewis's life:

  • 1898: Clive Staple Lewis born in Belfast, Ireland.
  • 1908: Lewis’s mother, Florence Augusta (‘Flora’) Hamilton Lewis, dies. Sent to the Wynyard School in Watford, Hertfordshire, England.
  • 1910: Enrolls as boarding student at Campbell College, Belfast, Ireland; leaves in December due to respiratory problems.
  • 1911: Enrolls at Cherboug House near Malvern College, England; abandons his Christian faith.
  • 1914: Meets Arthur Greeves, who becomes a lifelong friend. In September is privately tutored by W. T. "The Great Knock" Kirkpatrick.
  • 1916: Receives a scholarship to University College, Oxford.
  • 1917: Enlists in British Army.
  • 1918: Lewis’s close friend Paddy Moore reported killed in battle and Wounded in Battle of Arras.
  • 1918: Discharged from British Army.
  • 1919: Moves in with Moore’s mother, Mrs Janie King Moore, and sister, Maureen. Lewis publishes Spirits in Bondage under the pseudonym Clive Hamilton.
  • 1925: Appointed English Fellow of Magdalen College, Oxford, where he tutors English Language and Literature.
  • 1926: Meets friend and colleague J.R.R. Tolkien. Dymer under the pseudonym Clive Hamilton.Publishes Dymer under the pseudonym Clive Hamilton.
  • 1929: Lewis’s father, Albert Lewis, dies in Belfast amd abandons atheism and converts to theism.
  • 1930: Lewis, the Moores, and later Warren, Lewis's brother, move into "The Kilns".
  • 1931: Converts to Christianity.
  • 1933: The Pilgrim’s RegressPublishes The Pilgrim’s Regress.
  • 1936: Allegory of Love Publishes The Allegory of Love.
  • 1938: Out of the Silent Planet. Publishes the first novel in the Space Trilogy series, Out of the Silent Planet.
  • 1940: Weekly meetings of the "Inklings" begin and The Problem of Pain Publishes The Problem of Pain.
  • 1941: Mere Christianity Begins war-time broadcast talks on Christianity, later collected as Mere Christianity.
  • 1942: Attends the first meeting of Oxford University's Socratic Club. The Screwtape Letters Publishes The Screwtape Letters. broadcast TalksPublishes Broadcast Talks, based on BBC recordings. Paradise LostPublishes A Preface to Paradise Lost.
  • 1943: The Abolition of Man Publishes The Abolition of Man. Christian BehaviorPublishes Christian Behaviour, based on BBC recordings. Perelandra Publishes the second novel in the Space Trilogy series, Perelandra.
  • 1945: That Hideous Strength Publishes the third novel in the Space Trilogy series, That Hideous Strength.
  • 1946: The Great Divorce Publishes The Great Divorce. Lewis awarded an honorary Doctor of Divinity by the University of St. Andrews.
  • 1947: Miracles Publishes Miracles. Lewis appears on the cover of Time magazine.
  • 1948: Elected Fellow of the Royal Society of Literature.
  • 1949: The Weight of Glory and Other Addresses. Publishes The Weight of Glory and Other Addresses.
  • 1950: The Lion the Witch and the Wardrobe Publishes the first novel in the Chronicles of Narnia series, The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe.
  • 1951: Mrs. Moore dies. Prince Casbian Publishes the second novel in the Chronicles of Narnia series, Prince Caspian: The Return to Narnia.
  • 1952: Mere Christianity Publishes Mere Christianity, which combines previously published Broadcast Talks (1942), Christian Behavior (1943), and Beyond Personality (1944). In the Chronicles of Narnia series, The Voyage of the Dawn Treader. Publishes the third novel in the Chronicles of Narnia series, The Voyage of the Dawn Treader.
  • 1953: The Silver Chair Publishes the fourth novel in the Chronicles of Narnia series, The Silver Chair.
  • 1954: Becomes chair of Medieval and Renaissance Literature at Cambridge. The Horse and His Boy Publishes the fifth novel in the Chronicles of Narnia series, The Horse and His Boy. English Literature in the Sixteenth Century.Publishes English Literature in the Sixteenth Century.
  • 1955: The Magician's Nephew Publishes the sixth novel in the Chronicles of Narnia series, The Magician’s Nephew. Surprised By Joy Publishes Surprised By Joy.
  • 1956: The Last Battle Publishes the seventh, and final, novel in the Chronicles of Narnia series, The Last Battle. Marries Joy Davidman Gresham. Till We Have faces Publishes Till We Have Faces.
  • 1959: Becomes, with T. S. Eliot, a member of the Commission to Revise the Psalter.
  • 1960: Publishes The Four Loves Publishes The Four Loves. Lewis’s wife dies. Studies in Words Publishes Studies in Words.
  • 1961: Diagnosed with kidney inflammation. A Grief Observed Publishes A Grief Observed under the pseudonym N. W. Clerk. An Experiment in Criticism Publishes An Experiment in Criticism.
  • 1963: November 22, Lewis dies. Lewis died at 5:30 p.m. at The Kilns, one week before his 65th birthday on Friday, November 22; the same day on which President Kennedy was assassinated and Aldous Huxley died.
